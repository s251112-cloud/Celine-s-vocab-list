# Idiomatic Phrase Figurative Meaning HKDSE Context Example (with Modals) Done?
1 A drop in the ocean A very small, insignificant amount compared to what is needed. "Donating $100 to the charity fund can be helpful, but it must be noted that it is just a drop in the ocean given their massive deficit."
2 At the end of the day When everything is taken into consideration; ultimately. "You should always remember that, at the end of the day, your grades cannot define your entire worth as an individual."
3 Barking up the wrong tree Pursuing a mistaken line of thought or accusing the wrong person. "If you believe that closing down local playgrounds could solve the juvenile delinquency rate, you might be barking up the wrong tree."
4 Bite off more than you can chew To take on a task or responsibility that is way too big or difficult. "By joining three sports clubs and two debate teams, Chris may have bitten off more than he could chew."
5 Bite the bullet To face a difficult, unavoidable situation with courage and get it over with. "I really dread public speaking, but I must just bite the bullet and deliver the morning assembly speech."
6 Blessing in disguise Something that seems thoroughly bad at first, but results in a good outcome later. "Missing the school bus could have been a blessing in disguise, as I might have otherwise been trapped in severe traffic."
7 Burn the midnight oil To stay up exceptionally late working, revising, or studying hard. "Countless secondary school candidates ought to manage their time better so they do not have to burn the midnight oil right before exams."
8 Call it a day To stop working on an assignment or project for the rest of the 24-hour cycle. "We have been editing this video proposal for five hours straight. We should call it a day so that we can rest."
9 Cost an arm and a leg To be extremely expensive, requiring an unreasonable financial sacrifice. "Upgrading the school's computer lab with premium VR sets would certainly cost an arm and a leg."
